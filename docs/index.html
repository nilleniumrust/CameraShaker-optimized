SpringShaker

A physics-based camera shaker for Roblox built on a damped harmonic oscillator. Unlike noise-only shakers, SpringShaker simulates real mass and resistance — giving shakes a natural ring-down and weight.

Why SpringShaker?​

Traditional camera shakers scale Perlin noise by a fade envelope and call it a day. SpringShaker drives each shake through a damped harmonic oscillator — the same math that governs real pendulums and spring systems. The result is camera motion with genuine mass and resistance: explosions that ring out naturally, earthquakes that swell and bleed off over time, and impacts that feel physically grounded rather than scripted.

Performance​

At single instance usage, SpringShaker outperforms both RbxCameraShaker and Shake despite doing significantly more physics work per frame. At 10+ simultaneous instances the additional cost becomes measurable, but remains under 0.3ms at 250 instances — less than 2% of a 16ms frame budget.

At 1000 instances, SpringShaker maintains the tightest frame consistency of the three modules, with a stddev of 0.055ms vs Shake's 0.098ms. Consistent frametimes matter more than raw averages — spikes are what players actually notice.

Both --!native and --!optimize 2 are supported and tested. Native compilation alone provides a ~25x speedup per call and ~28% less peak memory at scale.

Easy to Setup​

Other camera shakers require manual CFrame management and a secondary Heartbeat connection to prevent camera drift at high framerates. SpringShaker handles render priority and camera application internally — there is nothing to configure and nothing to get wrong.

local SpringShaker = require(ReplicatedStorage.src.SpringShaker)
SpringShaker:ShakeSustained(SpringShaker:GetPreset("Earthquake"))

Memory​

SpringShaker uses more peak memory than alternatives due to richer per-instance physics state, but cleans up more completely after recycling. RbxCameraShaker retains ~200KB after garbage collection at 1000 instances. SpringShaker retains none.

Installation​

  • GitHub: Download latest release
  • Roblox Marketplace: SpringShaker

GitHub​

  1. Go to the latest release and download the .rbxm or .rbxmx file
  2. Insert it into your Roblox experience
  3. Place the package in ReplicatedStorage — do not place it in server-side datamodels.

Roblox Marketplace​

When you get the package, insert it from your toolbox directly. Put it anywhere, recommended: ReplicatedStorage ::: caution SpringShaker will not work when you put it into server-side datamodels. :::

Wally​

If you haven't installed Wally yet, go through wally installation process. Then add this to your wally.toml:

[dependencies]
SpringShaker = "nilleniumrust/springshaker@1.1.1"

Then run:

wally install
note

If you run into type errors with Wally packages, refer to wally-package-types. Both Wally and wally-package-types can be installed via Rokit.

Quick Start​

A sustained earthquake shake that runs until manually stopped:

local ReplicatedStorage = game:GetService("ReplicatedStorage")
local SpringShaker = require(ReplicatedStorage.src.SpringShaker)

-- Easy setup to get a preset. For example, we will choose Earthquake.
local Preset = SpringShaker:GetPreset("Earthquake")
SpringShaker:ShakeSustained(Preset)

-- A stop after 5 seconds, then a fade out time for 1 second!
task.delay(5, function()
    SpringShaker:HaltDurationWise(1)
end)
tip

:ShakeOnce() accepts the same preset or constructor as :ShakeSustained() — use it for one-shot shakes like explosions or impacts that fade out automatically.

Let's build our custom constructor, with its properties similar to a mortar explosion. We do know, our constructor .new() has it's own argumental properties that you can put into.

local ReplicatedStorage = game:GetService("ReplicatedStorage")
local SpringShaker = require(ReplicatedStorage.src.SpringShaker)

local mortar = SpringShaker.new({
    Magnitude = 5,         -- fairly strong, you'll feel it
    Roughness = 5,         -- medium speed noise, chaotic rumble character
    FadeInTime = 0,        -- instant shock, no buildup
    FadeOutTime = 1.8,     -- rumble lingers for ~2 seconds
    Tension = 122,         -- slow, heavy oscillation frequency
    Damping = 13,          -- rings out naturally over a few cycles
    RotationInfluence = Vector3.new(1.2, 0.5, 0.3), -- mostly pitch, some yaw, minimal roll
})

SpringShaker:ShakeOnce(mortar)
caution

Setting Magnitude above 10^5 risks Resonance — a real physics phenomenon where the forcing frequency matches the system's natural frequency, producing explosive and unregisterable values. SpringShaker will abort and warn if this is detected.
